# NekoServe v0.6.0 — "Teach the Results" pass

Released: 2026-04-14

v0.6.0 turns the Statistics Results page from a flat wall of KPIs into a guided, analytic, teaching-oriented experience. The core thesis of this release: **averages hide the story — show the distribution, the timeline, and the theory right next to the number**. The same numbers that used to sit in 11 plain cards now sit inside four themed sections, each surfacing a headline verdict, actionable bottleneck advice, distributions, time series, theory-vs-simulation comparison, clickable key moments, and inline glossary tooltips for every piece of terminology.

No simulator changes, no event schema changes, no breaking changes. v0.5.0 result JSON replays pixel-identically.

## 🏆 Hero Verdict + actionable bottleneck

The top of Results now shows a one-sentence headline answering the question every student actually has: **did this run go well, and if not, why?**

- **Auto-generated verdict** in three situations:
  - 🎉 `healthy` — abandon rate < 10 % and max utilization < 85 %
  - ⚠️ `strained` — abandon or ρ in the 10–30 % / 85–95 % range
  - 🚨 `overloaded` — abandon > 30 % or ρ ≥ 95 %
- **Two reading levels**: a 🐣 Beginner pill writes the verdict in plain language ("the café got hammered — 44 % walked out because staff was pinned at 100 %"); a 🎓 Pro pill swaps in ρ / λ / L terminology for classroom demos. Toggle lives in the Results header.
- **Situation-specific entrance animation**: the icon pulses green for healthy, wobbles amber for strained, and double-flashes red for overloaded. Each situation has its own `@keyframes` (respects `prefers-reduced-motion`).
- **BottleneckCallout** renders only when max ρ ≥ 85 % and gives **actionable advice** per bottleneck resource: "expand `seatCount` — adding staff does nothing because customers never reach the service phase". All six advice strings (3 resources × 2 levels) are i18n'd.

## 📊 4-section Results rebuild

The page is now organised around four themed `ResultsSection` cards that progressively reveal detail. Each section has an emoji icon, a one-line summary, the relevant KPI cards, new visualizations, and a "Tell me more ▼" expand showing Beginner or Pro theory.

1. **📊 Flow — arrivals & outcomes** — counts, plus a custom SVG Sankey-lite `FlowDiagram` showing how arrivals split into served / abandoned / in-flight, plus the clickable `KeyMomentsTimeline` (see below).
2. **⏱ Wait time decomposition** — avgWait KPIs, plus a horizontal stacked `StayBreakdown` (wait-seat + wait-order + dining + cat delay), plus the new `QueueTimeSeries` and `WaitHistogram` (see below), plus the existing WaitTimeChart.
3. **🔥 Utilization & bottleneck** — seat / staff / cat utilization KPIs, existing UtilizationChart, plus the new `UtilizationTimeSeries` (three stacked sparklines), plus `KingmanPrediction` (theory-vs-simulation callout).
4. **🐱 Cat interaction** — cat KPIs, existing CustomerPieChart, plus the new `StayDistribution` (cat-visited vs no-cat stay time histograms).

Every section slides in from below when it scrolls into the viewport via `IntersectionObserver`.

## 🎞 Count-up animations + Recharts entrance

- **`useCountUp` hook** (`src/renderer/src/hooks/useCountUp.ts`) — rAF-driven easeOutQuart number ticker that respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- **`<AnimatedNumber>` component** — wraps `useCountUp` and gates the animation on an `IntersectionObserver` so numbers only tick when they actually enter the viewport (not burn through below the fold).
- **`KpiCard` extended** — accepts `numeric` + `decimals` + `numericSuffix` props that route the value through AnimatedNumber. The old `value` string path stays for back-compat.
- **Recharts animations re-enabled**: UtilizationChart, WaitTimeChart, and CustomerPieChart all now animate their bars / slices on mount (`isAnimationActive`, `animationDuration={800}`, `animationEasing='ease-out'`).

## 🔍 Distributions, time series, and key moments

A new batch of visualizations derived from the raw event log.

### `QueueTimeSeries` — L(t) over the whole run

Mini SVG polyline showing queue length across every sim-minute of the run, with a pulse-ringed peak marker and a natural-language annotation ("queue peaked at 12 people around minute 134"). Pairs with Little's Law in the Section 2 expand detail — the curve visually *is* the L that the formula refers to.

### `WaitHistogram` — per-customer wait time distribution

10-bin horizontal histogram of individual `waitForSeat` times (bin count actually scales as `sqrt(N)` clamped to [8, 16] so calm runs don't look sparse). Mean is marked with a dashed red line. The histogram makes it immediately obvious why "avgWait = 15 min" hides half the customers waiting much less and half waiting much more.

### `KeyMomentsTimeline` — clickable narrative timeline

Small timeline axis at the top of Section 1 that auto-surfaces up to 5 key moments:

- 🌅 First customer arrival
- 🔥 Queue peak (only when ≥ 2 people — calm runs don't show trivial "peaks")
- ❌ First abandon (only when any abandon happened)
- 👥 Staff peak busy (only when ≥ 2 staff busy simultaneously)
- ✅ Last customer served

**Moments cluster when close together**: two moments within 8 % of the axis get stacked vertically with a connector stem, three or more collapse into a single merged bubble with a `+N` badge and a tooltip listing all kinds. A "店內平順運作 / Quiet running" label appears in any gap wider than 40 % of the axis so calm stretches read as "nothing happened here, it was fine".

**Bubbles are clickable**: clicking any bubble fires `onJumpToPlayback(simTime)` which App.tsx routes to `setPlaybackTime + setPage('playback')`, seeking the shared cursor to that moment and switching to the Playback page. Students can then see what the café looked like at that exact instant instead of just reading a summary.

### `UtilizationTimeSeries` — three resource sparklines

Three stacked row charts (seat / staff / cat) showing how each resource's utilization evolved across the run. Each row has a label column on the left, a filled area plot, a dashed vertical peak marker, and an average + peak annotation on the right. Answers the question that a single ρ KPI can't: "was staff busy the whole day, or was it a calm morning and a brutal afternoon?"

### `StayDistribution` — cat-visited vs not, side by side

Side-by-side histograms of total stay time split by whether the customer met a cat. Two cohorts, two mean lines, one delta annotation ("with-cat average 48.2 min, without 41.7 min — delta 6.5 min ≈ one interaction duration"). Directly validates the Section 4 thesis that cat visits inflate stay time. Left label column prevents the row titles from being overlapped by tall histogram bars.

## 🧪 Theory vs simulation — Kingman prediction

A new `KingmanPrediction` callout in Section 3 runs Kingman's single-station approximation

```
W_q ≈ (ρ / (1 − ρ)) · ((C_a² + C_s²) / 2) · E[S]
```

against the simulator's config (λ = 1/arrivalInterval, E[S] = orderTime + prepTime, c = staffCount, Poisson + exponential baseline) and compares the predicted queue wait against the simulator's actual `avgWaitForOrder`. Three severity tones:

- **< 20 % gap** — green "theory holds"
- **20–50 % gap** — amber "theory starts to diverge"
- **≥ 50 % gap or ρ ≥ 1** — red "theory fails, and that's the whole point of DES"

The divergence is the teaching moment: Kingman assumes Poisson arrivals, exponential service, no abandonment, no cat delays — NekoServe has all of those complications baked in. Seeing theory miss by 50 % is exactly why discrete-event simulation exists as a tool separate from pure queueing theory.

## 📚 Inline glossary tooltips

Every DES / queueing-theory term that appears in Results prose now has a **click/hover tooltip** showing its label and a plain-language definition. No more blank stares at "Kingman" or "ρ".

14 terms covered in both languages:

- **Kingman's approximation** — the formula, its form, and its history
- **Little's Law** — the L = λW identity and its universal applicability
- **Poisson process** — "random but predictable on average"
- **Utilization ρ** — the 0–100 % resource busyness and the 85 % cliff
- **Arrival rate λ** — average customers per time unit
- **W_q, L(t), L̄** — queue wait / length over time / time-averaged length
- **M/M/c model** — the notation decoded
- **SimPy + env.run** — the Python framework and its clock truncation
- **DES** — what Discrete Event Simulation is
- **Exponential distribution** — memorylessness explained
- **Erlang-C** — the exact multi-server wait formula
- **In-flight customers** — what the "still-in-shop-at-end" residual means
- **Bottleneck** — the concept plus why only expanding it helps

**Technical implementation**:

- **`glossary.ts`** utility carries the term list, a length-sorted keyword index, and a `splitByTerms(text)` function that runs any string through a compiled regex and returns an array of text / term segments.
- **`<TermTooltip>`** component renders a span with a dotted underline, `tabIndex={0}` for keyboard focus, and `role="button"` for accessibility.
- The popover itself is **rendered via React portal at `document.body`** with `position: fixed`, so it completely escapes any overflow-clipped or scrollable ancestor container. Previously the popover was absolutely positioned inside the trigger and got clipped when the term was near the top of the scroll viewport — this was fixed before the release.
- **Auto-flip placement**: `useLayoutEffect` measures the trigger's `getBoundingClientRect` and decides `above` or `below` based on available space, so terms near the viewport top flip their popover downward.
- **Horizontal clamp**: the popover center is clamped to viewport bounds so it never overflows the left or right edge.
- **`pointer-events: none`** on the popover prevents flicker when the mouse crosses from trigger to popover.
- **`renderWithTerms(text)`** helper is applied to: HeroVerdict message, BottleneckCallout advice + hint, KingmanPrediction verdict / title / hint, and all 8 section expand prose slots (4 sections × Beginner/Pro).

## ⬆️ Upgrade notes

- Version bumped `0.5.0` → `0.6.0`.
- **No breaking changes**. No reducer state changes, no event schema changes, no config field changes, no metric field changes. v0.5.0 result JSON replays identically.
- `MetricSummary` and `SimulationResult` shapes are unchanged; all new visualizations derive from the existing fields plus a new pass through `eventLog`.
- `KpiCard` gained three new optional props (`numeric`, `decimals`, `numericSuffix`). Existing call sites that pass `value: string` still work unchanged.
- `ResultsPage` gained a new optional `onJumpToPlayback?: (simTime: number) => void` prop wired from `App.tsx`. Null-safe — if the prop is absent the KeyMomentsTimeline bubbles just don't react to clicks.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` now disables: count-up animations (value jumps straight to final), Recharts entrance animations (via intrinsic Recharts behaviour), Hero Verdict pulse, ResultsSection slide-in, and FlowDiagram particle flow.
