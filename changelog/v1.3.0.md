# NekoServe v1.3.0 -- Literature review, Dbeis ρ_R correction, and interactive formula teaching

Released: 2026-04-22

v1.3.0 is a major academic-rigor release. It responds to feedback from Professor Chu Kuo-Chung (2026-04-19) that the 14 parameters were "observational assumptions" rather than "literature-backed curve fits". The release adds a full literature review, cites every parameter to its empirical source, corrects the utilization formula per Dbeis & Al-Sahili (2024), and makes every formula in the app teach itself through clickable symbol chips and hover glossary tooltips.

## Literature review module (2.1–2.5)

A new five-card literature review section lives in the Learning Notes sidebar (before the existing References card):

- **2.1 Discrete-event simulation & queueing theory foundations** — Ancker & Gafarian 1963 (I, II), Little 1961.
- **2.2 Empirical service-system studies in F&B** — Hasugian 2020 (Weibull arrivals, Normal service, EasyFit curve fitting) vs Dbeis 2024 (Poisson arrivals, Log-Normal service, 123-hour drive-thru observation, chi-square validation).
- **2.3 Why cat cafés need their own model** — Hirsch et al. 2025 (227-hour Stockholm cat café observation, 27 cats, 70 days, cat-human interaction only 55.6% of observed time), Li et al. 2025 (PLS-SEM on 423 pet café customers — coffee quality insignificant, pet interactivity dominant), Ropski et al. 2023 (797 cats across foster vs café settings).
- **2.4 Research gap & positioning** — the three gaps (A: cat-café empirics not connected to queueing systems; B: F&B simulations lack stochastic non-fixed resources; C: balking/reneging not validated in animal-present contexts) and NekoServe's "bottleneck-dominance effect" observation.
- **2.5 Math correction: Dbeis 2024 ρ_R formula** — classical ρ = λ/μ vs corrected ρ_R = (λ − RR)/μ, with the full RCRF = -2.056S + 1.37·eˢ - 1.173 nonlinear regression model.

Every card carries per-section citations with DOI links and APA-style formatting. Content is written in Ruru's first-person voice (not "the present author"), consistent with the rest of the app's copy.

## Utilization formula correction (ρ_R)

Dbeis & Al-Sahili (2024) showed that the classical utilization formula ρ = λ/μ produces false "system collapse" readings (ρ > 1) when customers renege heavily, because it double-counts customers who never actually consumed server time. Their correction:

```
ρ_R = (λ − RR) / μ
```

NekoServe's engine now surfaces six new metrics:

- `arrivalRate` (λ, customers/min) — computed as total arrivals ÷ sim duration
- `renegingRate` (RR, customers/min) — computed as total abandons ÷ sim duration
- `serviceRate` (μ, customers/min per server) — computed as 1 / (orderTime + preparationTime)
- `meanServiceTime` (E[S], minutes)
- `rhoClassical` — classical M/M/c utilization λ / (c · μ)
- `rhoCorrected` — Dbeis 2024 corrected utilization (λ − RR) / (c · μ)

The time-based utilization (`seatUtilization` / `staffUtilization` / `catUtilization`) is preserved as the engine's measured truth. The three forms are displayed side-by-side in a new `RhoCorrectionPanel` on the Results page. When classical ρ ≥ 1 but ρ_R < 1, the panel raises a "⚠ 古典公式誤判為崩潰" warning banner so students can see the misreading in action.

## Parameter-to-literature tooltips (13 core parameters)

Every core parameter's "📘 Design rationale" panel now cites an empirical source as its first reference (before the theoretical-framework references that were already there):

- `seatCount`, `staffCount`, `catCount`, `catIdleInterval`, `catRestProbability`, `catRestDuration`, `catInteractionTime` → Hirsch et al. 2025 (10.3390/ani15223233)

- `orderTime`, `preparationTime`, `diningTime` → Dbeis 2024
- `maxWaitTime` → Ancker 1963a (10.1287/opre.11.1.88) + Dbeis 2024

DOIs link directly to `https://doi.org/{doi}` via the existing Electron external-browser handler. 13 parameters × 2 locales = 26 i18n edits.

## Interactive formula teaching (chips + hover glossary)

Every formula in the app now teaches itself through two complementary mechanisms:

### Clickable symbol chips (`InteractiveFormula`)

The Design rationale panel for 11 parameters with formulas (all except `catCount`, `maxWaitTime`, `randomSeed`, `warmUpDuration`) now renders each symbol (`T`, `λ`, `μ`, `σ`, `p`, `X`, `Exp(λ)`, `N(μ, σ²)`, `Bernoulli(p)`, `c_{seat}`, `c_{staff}`, `T_{run}`) as a clickable chip. Clicking pops out a card with the symbol's label, plain-language description, unit, and a concrete example — identical UX to the Results page's formula teaching, now shared across pages.

A 13-entry shared symbol dictionary lives under `settings:formulaParts.*` and is reused across all parameter formulas. The existing `InteractiveFormula` component was extended with `i18nNs` / `i18nBasePath` props so it can read from either the `results` or `settings` namespace.

### Always-visible 🔍 plain-language hint

Below each formula (in both the Design rationale and the Learning Notes), a one-sentence 🔍 caption translates the LaTeX into everyday words — for example `T ~ Exp(λ), λ = 1/8` gets the hint "interarrival time T follows an Exponential distribution with rate λ = 1/8 (0.125 customers per minute) — one customer every 8 minutes on average". 11 formulaHint strings per locale.

### Deep-dive `FormulaExplain`

A new `FormulaExplain` component wraps `BlockMath` with a 🔍 hint line and an optional "更多說明 ▼" expander for extended explanations. Used in the 2.1 and 2.5 literature cards for Little's Law, classical ρ, Dbeis ρ_R, and the RCRF regression.

## Glossary expansion (12 new terms)

The shared glossary used by the `TermTooltip` hover system grew by 12 entries, all available bilingually:

- `reneging`, `balking`, `Log-Normal`, `Weibull`, `RCRF`, `ρ_R`, `Normal distribution`, `curve fitting`, `M/M/1`, `PLS-SEM`, `chi-square`, `agent-based`

The glossary is now also wired into:

- **Design rationale panels** (`ParamRationale`): the meaning / why / theory / defaultBasis text is run through `renderWithTerms`, so any glossary keyword gets auto-wrapped with a hover-tooltip.
- **Literature review cards**: key terms in the 2.1–2.5 prose (Poisson, M/M/c, balking, reneging, DES, PLS-SEM, chi-square, Log-Normal, curve fitting, etc.) are explicitly wrapped with a new `<Term k="...">` JSX helper exported from `learnContent/shared.tsx`.

Every tooltip popup continues to render via a React portal to `document.body` with `position: fixed`, so it escapes overflow-clipped parents and flips above/below based on viewport space.

## Engine, contracts, and export alignment

- **Python simulator** (`simulator-python/simulator/core.py`): metrics dict extended with the six new ρ_R fields, all with docstring references to Dbeis 2024. The pre-built binary under `simulator-python/dist/simulator/simulator` was rebuilt via PyInstaller so production and dev both emit the new fields.
- **TypeScript contract** (`shared/contracts/types.ts`): `MetricSummary` interface extended with the six new fields.
- **CSV export** (`src/renderer/src/utils/export.ts`): `METRIC_KEYS` / `METRIC_HEADERS` updated so CSVs include the new columns. Also filled in the previously-missing `avgCatVisitsPerCustomer` and `noCatVisitRate` headers.
- **Defensive rendering**: `RhoCorrectionPanel` guards against older simulator binaries via an `fmt()` helper that returns "—" instead of crashing on `undefined.toFixed(...)`. A ⚠ banner alerts users when the engine is out-of-date.

## Author's note on methodology

The underlying thesis is that design-thinking simulation should be transparent about its assumptions. v1.3.0 is the first release where every non-trivial parameter can be traced to either (a) an empirical cat-café study, (b) a curve-fit restaurant study, or (c) a classical queueing-theory result — and where the critique "your ρ says the system collapses" can be met with "yes, the classical formula does, but Dbeis 2024's correction explains why it runs fine anyway". The 14-parameter simulator did not change shape; the framing around it did.
